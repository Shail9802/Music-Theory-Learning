All About Modes

The Ionian mode is the first and most familiar mode of the major scale. It is also known as the major scale, and has a happy, bright, and cheerful sound. It is characterized by its pattern of whole and half steps: W-W-H-W-W-W-H (W = whole step, H = half step). It is widely used in Western classical music, pop, rock, and many other genres.

The Dorian mode is the second mode of the major scale and is characterized by its distinct minor sound. It has a melancholic and introspective quality, often used in jazz and rock music. Its unique pattern of whole and half steps is: W-H-W-W-W-H-W (W = whole step, H = half step). The Dorian mode is often used to create a sense of tension and release in music, and is frequently employed in modal jazz and blues progressions.

The Phrygian mode is the third mode of the major scale and has a distinctive Spanish or Middle Eastern sound. It is characterized by its minor tonality and a flattened second scale degree, which gives it a unique and exotic character. Its pattern of whole and half steps is: H-W-W-W-H-W-W (W = whole step, H = half step). The Phrygian mode is often used in flamenco, progressive rock, and metal music to create a dark and mysterious atmosphere.

The Lydian mode is the fourth mode of the major scale and has a bright and uplifting sound. It is characterized by its raised fourth scale degree, which creates a sense of tension and dissonance that is often resolved by returning to the tonic note. Its pattern of whole and half steps is: W-W-W-H-W-W-H (W = whole step, H = half step). The Lydian mode is often used in jazz and rock music to create a dreamy and ethereal atmosphere.

The Mixolydian mode is the fifth mode of the major scale and has a bluesy and rock 'n' roll sound. It is characterized by its flattened seventh scale degree, which gives it a dominant or "resolving" quality. Its pattern of whole and half steps is: W-W-H-W-W-H-W (W = whole step, H = half step). The Mixolydian mode is often used in blues, rock, and country music to create a feeling of tension and release.

TheAeolian mode is the sixth mode of the major scale and is also known as the natural minor scale. It has a melancholic and somber quality, often used in classical and metal music. Its pattern of whole and half steps is: W-H-W-W-H-W-W (W = whole step, H = half step). The Aeolian mode is often used to create a sense of sadness or emotional depth in music.

The Locrian mode is the seventh and last mode of the major scale, and has a very unstable and unresolved sound. It is characterized by its flattened second and fifth scale degrees, which give it a dissonant and diminished quality. Its pattern of whole and half steps is: H-W-W-H-W-W-W (W = whole step, H = half step). The Locrian mode is rarely used in traditional Western music due to its unstable tonality, but is occasionally employed in jazz and experimental music.
